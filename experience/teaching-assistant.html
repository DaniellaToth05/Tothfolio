Overview

I have worked as an Undergraduate Teaching Assistant for multiple core computer science courses at the University of Guelph. In this role, I support students during labs, office hours, and assessments while helping them develop confidence in programming and problem-solving.

TAing has strengthened my communication skills and reinforced my own understanding of foundational software concepts by explaining them clearly to others.

CIS*1500 — Introduction to Programming

I worked as a Teaching Assistant for CIS*1500 across two terms, supporting students who were often encountering programming for the first time.

  • Assisted students during weekly labs and office hours
  • Helped debug C programs involving loops, arrays, functions, and pointers
  • Explained core programming concepts in a clear and approachable way
  • Worked with other TAs and instructors to support student success

This role helped me learn how to break down complex ideas and adapt explanations based on a student’s level of understanding.

CIS*2250 — Software Design II

I am currently a Teaching Assistant for CIS*2250, a course focused on the process of software design and the application of best practices for developing and reviewing software in a collaborative setting.

The course places a strong emphasis on teamwork, communication, and responsibility in software development. Students work in pairs and small groups on design challenges and a substantial team-based software project, using Python as the implementation language.

  • Support students during in-person labs focused on pair and group work
  • Assist students with Python-based lab work and project development
  • Help students reason about software design decisions and trade-offs
  • Grade labs, assignments, and exams and return feedback within required timelines
  • Hold weekly office hours and respond to student questions through course communication tools
  • Participate in regular TA meetings and assist with exam invigilation

Supporting CIS*2250 has strengthened my ability to communicate software design concepts clearly and to guide students in reflecting on collaboration, design responsibility, and effective development practices.

Key Takeaways

Being a Teaching Assistant has improved my technical communication skills, patience, and confidence. It has also reinforced the importance of writing clear, maintainable code that others can understand.
